Ph.D @ University of Cambridge

We study inverse problems in fluid dynamics and magnetic resonance velocimetry (MRV). Starting from a qualitatively accurate physical model for a particular fluid flow, we use MRV measurements to revise the model, rendering it quantitatively accurate. Then, the revised model is used to infer hidden quantities of the fluid or the flow, which cannot be measured directly (e.g. viscosity or wall-shear stress). At the same time, we obtain a denoised and higher-resolution version of the original MRV signal.

The above methodologies combine fluid mechanics with optimal control theory and Bayesian inference. We intent to apply them to improve the examination of cardiovascular diseases by reducing the MRV scanning times and increase the resolution of blood flow measurements.
